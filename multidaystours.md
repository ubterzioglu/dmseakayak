## 7-Day Mediterranean Kayaking Adventure

*A mix of sea, sunshine, ancient ruins, and hidden beaches — all by kayak!*

### Day 1 | Arrival in Kaş

Welcome to Kaş! It's a laid-back coastal town with turquoise waters, mountain views, and cozy little cafes. After you arrive at your accommodation (camp site), wander the narrow streets, grab a sunset drink, and get ready for your kayaking adventure to kick off the next day. Don't get carried away with drinks too much!

### Day 2 | Kaş to Üzümlü Beach — 15 km

Time to hit the water! You'll paddle west along the beautiful Kaş coastline. Expect rocky cliffs, hidden coves, crystal-clear swimming spots, and that epic Mediterranean blue all day long. After about 15 km, you'll arrive at Üzümlü Beach — a peaceful, usually uncrowded bay that's perfect for a chill end to your first paddling day.

### Day 3 | Kalkan to Patara — 14 km

We transfer over to Kalkan in the morning — a postcard-perfect little harbor town. From there, paddle along rugged coastlines toward **Patara**, famous for its huge stretch of sandy beach and ancient ruins tucked behind the dunes. It's a nice blend of sea adventure and history, with plenty of open coastline to enjoy. You will be able to visit the ancient city of Patara with its parliament building and theater and more! We will transfer you to Üçağız and you will get to enjoy the evening in Üçağız village.

### Day 4 | Kekova Classic Route — 8 km

Today, we head to **Kekova**, a paddling paradise! Today's route is laid-back and beginner-friendly, but packed with history. You'll glide over the famous **Sunken City of Kekova**, where ancient staircases and harbor walls lie beneath the surface — visible through the clear waters. We'll stop off at **Simena**, the tiny castle village you can only reach by kayak or boat. Explore the castle, enjoy some of the best views around, and maybe try the homemade ice cream before paddling back to Üçağız.

### Day 5 | Kekova West Route — 14 km

We're covering more ground today! This route is for those who enjoy a full day of paddling, with a side of ancient ruins and hidden coves. You'll paddle from Üçağız toward the wild, western side of Kekova, passing tucked-away bays and rugged cliffs. One of the highlights is reaching **Aperlai**, a lesser-known ancient Lycian city. You'll hop out for a short hike along the **Lycian Way**, soak up some history, and snorkel above sunken ruins. If there is enough wind, you may even get to enjoy kayak sailing!

### Day 6 | Kekova East Route — 15 km

Today's route is in the east side of Kekova, and it's packed with cool spots. You'll glide over ancient ruins hidden in the shallows, paddle past islands, and follow the coast toward **Andriake**, the ancient port of Myra. Keep your eyes peeled for sea turtles and wild goats along the way. The biggest highlight will be at the end of the tour following the fresh water stream of Andriake, you will be spotting sea turtles! You will be staying in Andriake for the night and enjoy a relaxed evening at your camp site. You can keep enjoying the sandy beach of Andriake as long as you want after kayaking.

### Day 7 | Andriake to Taşdibi Beach — 14 km

The last leg of the adventure! You'll start from **Andriake**, cruise along the coast, enjoy your final swims, and wrap things up at **Taşdibi Beach**, a wide, pebbly bay that's perfect for celebrating your journey. It's a chilled-out finish to an epic week on the Mediterranean.

**Quick Highlights:**

- Paddle hidden bays, beaches & ancient ruins
- Snorkel over sunken cities
- Hike along the Lycian Way
- Wildlife spotting: sea turtles, wild goats, maybe monk seals
- Fresh local lunches (optional) & castle village stops
- Small-group, guide-led adventure with plenty of swim breaks

## Lycian Kayak & Comfort Escape (FINAL)

**An all-inclusive paddling experience through history and nature!**

Embark on a journey through time and nature with our weekly sea kayaking tour along Turkey's stunning Lycian Coast. Paddle through crystal-clear waters while discovering ancient cities, hidden coves, and captivating historical ruins that date back millennia. From the forgotten wonders of Lycian tombs carved into cliffs to the remains of ancient amphitheaters, every stroke brings you closer to the rich history that shaped this legendary region.

Immerse yourself in a unique cultural experience, where each stop reveals a new chapter in the story of the Lycians, and enjoy the serenity of a coastline untouched by mass tourism. After each adventure, savor the exquisite flavors of renowned Turkish cuisine, offering a delightful culinary journey to complement your unforgettable experiences of each day.

Perfect for adventurers, history enthusiasts, and those seeking a truly authentic and unforgettable experience. Join us for a sea kayaking adventure like no other, where the past and present flow together in perfect harmony.

### Day 1: Arrival and Introduction to Kekova

Upon arrival at the airport, you will be warmly greeted by our team and transferred to the picturesque village of Üçağız. En route, you will enjoy your first Turkish lunch while taking in the landscapes of the region. Once settled into your hotel in Üçağız/Simena, you will embark on your first kayaking experience, paddling through the ancient ruins of the Sunken City of Kekova and exploring the historic and picturesque island of Kaleköy, home to a Lycian/Roman castle. The day will conclude with a traditional Turkish dinner at a local restaurant, offering a perfect opportunity to unwind and reflect on the adventure ahead.

**Paddle distance: 3 miles**

### Day 2: Aperlai & Snorkeling Adventure

Begin your day with a traditional Turkish breakfast before paddling along the western route to the ancient city of Aperlai, stopping at scenic bays like Tersane Bay along the way. A brief hike offers breathtaking views of the ruins of Aperlai, nestled in a remote corner of the coastline and reachable only via sea or hiking on the Lycian trail. Listen to your guide explaining one of the most sought after trade items of Aperlai in the ancient times: the Royal purple dye made out of murex snails. After walking in and around the ruins, get your snorkel and mask to explore the submerged ruins of Aperlai Sunken City along the shoreline—one of the region's most unique underwater archaeological sites.

Walk back to Nomad Ramazan's place in Polemos bay and enjoy a fresh seafood lunch and probably the best fries in the area at his fisherman house. Paddle back following the shoreline towards Üçağız soaking in the unspoiled views of the coast. If the conditions are favorable, you can also enjoy an exhilarating sailing experience with your kayak, harnessing the power of the wind and just relaxing and embracing the subtle waves and charming vistas of the coast.

**Paddle distance: 8.5 miles | Hiking: 1.5 miles**

### Day 3: Kekova East Route & Andriake Lycian Museum

Following a traditional Turkish breakfast, we set off for the eastern route of Kekova, discovering hidden bays and secluded beaches. Along the way, visit a protected cave known for its use by endangered monk seals as a nesting ground, and be on the look out and take the opportunity to spot sea turtles swimming in the crystal-clear waters. Towards the end of the route, paddle into the fresh water stream of Andriake and prepare yourselves to be welcomed by turtles leisurely feeding and gliding in the stream. After a day of kayaking, we visit the Andriake Lycian Museum and the ancient cistern, where you'll gain deeper insights into the rich Lycian heritage. Later, we transfer to Kaş, a vibrant coastal town renowned for its relaxed atmosphere and spectacular views. Enjoy a delicious dinner at one of the town's top restaurants after checking into your hotel.

**Paddle distance: 9 miles**

### Day 4: Free day for exploration with options of your own choice

After a leisurely breakfast, enjoy a free day to explore the area at your own pace. You can opt for a boat ride to the Greek island of Kastellorizo, where you'll discover its charming harbor, colorful streets, and blend of Greek and Turkish culture. Alternatively, relax in Kaş, visit its beaches, or wander through the town's quaint streets. For those with a passion for history, we offer an optional excursion to Myra and the Church of Saint Nicholas, while diving enthusiasts can experience the crystal-clear waters of Kaş, a world-renowned diving destination. End the day with a delicious dinner featuring Mediterranean flavors.

### Day 5: Kayaking in the Kaş Archipelago

After breakfast, embark on a full day of kayaking around the stunning coastlines of Kaş. Paddle through hidden coves, enjoy the serenity of the Mediterranean, and take in the spectacular coastal view of Kaş and Kastellorizo.

A leisurely lunch at a seaside restaurant provides an opportunity to relax and refuel before continuing your kayaking adventure. In the evening, savor a traditional Turkish dinner at a renowned local restaurant, enjoying the fresh ingredients that define the region's cuisine.

**Paddle distance: 7.5 miles**

### Day 6: Patara Adventure

After checking out of your hotel, transfer to Kalkan for a day of exploration. Paddle along the coast to Patara Beach, home to Turkey's longest stretch of sand and the site of one of the world's earliest parliamentary buildings. Enjoy a beachside picnic lunch and take time to explore the ancient ruins of Patara, including Roman theaters and historical remnants. After a day full of history and paddling adventure, we transfer you to your hotel in Patara for a well-earned farewell dinner, reflecting on the experiences of the day.

**Paddle distance: 9 miles**

### Day 7: Departure

On your final day, enjoy a leisurely breakfast before checking out of your hotel. Our team will transfer you to the airport, where you will depart with memories of an unforgettable sea kayaking journey along the stunning Lycian Coast.

### Destination Guide

**Kekova**

Kekova is a place where time seems to pause, revealing a captivating blend of ancient mystery and natural wonder. Tucked along Turkey's turquoise coast, this hidden paradise is home to the mesmerizing sunken city of Dolchiste, an ancient Lycian settlement partially submerged following a series of earthquakes during the 2nd century. Paddle through this historical wonder in your kayak, glide over centuries-old stone walls, and hear whispers of the past as you explore this unique underwater world. Beyond its historical allure, Kekova's untouched bays and serene landscapes make it a haven of tranquility. Step into a world like no other – where legends sleep and nature reigns supreme.

**Simena**

Perched on the turquoise shores of the Lycian Coast, Simena is a charming castle village that blends history, natural beauty, and tranquil Mediterranean charm. Dominated by a striking ancient Lycian castle, the village offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding bays and islands. Accessible only by sea or on foot, Simena is a hidden gem where you can wander narrow stone paths, admire traditional whitewashed houses adorned with bougainvillea, and discover rock-cut tombs. This idyllic destination is the perfect escape for travelers seeking a glimpse into Turkey's past while soaking in its serene coastal allure.

**Aperlai**

Hidden along Turkey's Lycian Coast, the ancient city of Aperlai offers a fascinating journey into history and nature. Once a thriving Lycian settlement, its ruins now rest quietly by the water, partially submerged beneath the turquoise sea. Visitors can explore remnants of city walls, sarcophagi, and the unique sunken ruins, creating a captivating blend of archaeology and natural beauty. Accessible only by boat or kayak, Aperlai is an ideal destination for adventurers seeking a serene escape and a glimpse into the ancient world amidst breathtaking coastal scenery.

**Andriake**

Andriake, the ancient harbor of Myra in Lycia, was a bustling center of maritime trade and cultural exchange. Located on Turkey's stunning southern coast, this historic port played a pivotal role in the ancient Mediterranean economy, connecting Asia Minor to the wider Roman Empire. Visitors today can explore its well-preserved granaries, built under Emperor Hadrian, and learn about its significance as a hub for grain storage and trade. Perfect for history enthusiasts and adventurers alike, Andriake offers a glimpse into the dynamic life of an ancient seafaring civilization. Dive into history and discover Andriake's timeless charm!

**Kaş**

Nestled along Turkey's stunning Mediterranean coastline, Kaş is a charming coastal town that blends natural beauty with rich history. Renowned for its turquoise waters, rugged cliffs, and hidden coves, Kaş is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. From world-class diving and sea kayaking to exploring ancient Lycian ruins and vibrant local markets, the town offers something for everyone. Its laid-back atmosphere, colorful streets, and delicious Mediterranean cuisine make Kaş the perfect destination to relax, explore, and immerse yourself in the beauty of the Turkish Riviera.

**Patara**

Patara, one of the most captivating destinations on Turkey's Lycian Coast, is a treasure trove of history and natural beauty. Home to the longest sandy beach in Turkey, this ancient city is steeped in rich heritage as the birthplace of democracy, boasting ruins like the grand Roman theater, an impressive ancient lighthouse that is being resurrected, and the iconic Lycian parliament building that served as the first parliament building in the known human history. Walking through Patara's well-preserved streets, you'll feel transported to a bygone era, while the pristine beach and serene atmosphere offer a perfect backdrop for exploration and relaxation.
